## Post Text

A few weeks ago I told my team that AI needs to do 92% of their work or they'll get left behind.
Here’s how we're doing it (and why):
Step 1: Get ChatGPT Plus/Pro
Step 2: Create your master prompt
• Tell AI: "I'm [your role] at [company type]. Create a master prompt for me. Ask me every question you need to give me the most context possible."
• Spend 30-45 minutes answering everything it asks
• Save the output as a PDF
• Upload this to every new chat so AI knows your full context
Step 3: Build system prompts
Master prompts tell AI who you are. System prompts tell AI HOW to work.
Here's the process:
• Ask AI to create any output (email, ad, report)
• Keep refining until it's perfect (3-6 iterations)
• Then ask: "Write the system prompt that would have generated this output"
• Save that prompt - it's now your intellectual property
Now you have the exact formula to get that quality every time.
Step 4: Use project folders
Think of these like rooms in your office with all context on the walls.
• Create a project for each major area of your life/business
• Upload your master prompt + all relevant documents
• Every conversation builds on previous context
• Share folders with your team for instant knowledge transfer
I use this for investment decisions, business strategy, even family planning.
Step 5: Set your custom instructions
This makes AI remember how you like outputs formatted.
Go to Settings → Personalization → Custom Instructions:
• Tell it your communication style (short, bullet points, no fluff)
• Remove AI language like "delve" and "moreover"
• Set your default tone and format preferences
Never repeat formatting requests again.
Step 6: Turn everything into custom GPTs
These are your AI employees that do specific tasks consistently.
• Take your best system prompts
• Create custom GPTs for each repeatable task
• Share them with your team
• Update once, everyone gets the improvement
I have custom GPTs for: emails, content creation, financial analysis, hiring, strategy docs.
Step 7: Refine and improve
Use AI to teach you AI.
• Ask it to create your master prompt
• Ask it to write your system prompts
• Ask it to suggest custom instructions
• Ask it to help you build better prompts
Here's what 92% actually looks like:
- Content: AI does research, outlines, first drafts. You edit and add your voice.
- Operations: AI creates SOPs, analyzes processes, suggests improvements. You decide.
- Finance: AI analyzes reports, creates models, finds insights. You make decisions.
- Strategy: AI processes information, suggests options. You choose direction.
The 8% that stays human: Vision, taste, final decisions, and emotional intelligence.
My team went from thinking AI was "kind of helpful" to saying it's their most valuable employee.
It could be yours too.

Dan Martell I have to disagree with your final conclusion, not out of theory, but out of practical experience. I am a thinking and writing engineer who has always spent a lot of time to contemplate and work through ideas before I look at other opinions of references. You make the statement:
- Content: AI does research, outlines, first drafts. You edit and add your voice.
- Operations: AI creates SOPs, analyzes processes, suggests improvements. You decide.
- Finance: AI analyzes reports, creates models, finds insights. You make decisions.
- Strategy: AI processes information, suggests options. You choose direction.
I disagree. On all of these topics it is essential that the human takes the first step and that you apply your thinking BEFORE you consult the AI. As Daniel Kahneman define in his book "Thinking Fast and Slow", the human brain will default to the easy way of doing things, so if you first ask for an answer from the AI, the chance is very slim that you will come up with anything better, more creative or with more insight. Our "generally lazy" brains will revert to the first answer that is good enough. But, what is worse is that you just told your brain "It is OK, you do not have to, the AI will do it." and at that exact moment you can just as well fire that employee, since the AI just won by being first in line and effectively telling the human how to think
I would change you suggestion to the following:
- Content: I think what I want to achieve. I write it on paper, draw diagrams and relate it to what I know. When I have developed a solid understanding, I MIGHT use the AI to do research and combine the research with my notes to generate a first drafts. Then I edit my document and ask the AI to optimize the appearance, structure and language.
- Operations: I look at the operations and evaluate it against current trends, the data and the observed bottlenecks. I also talk to people to determine challenges that might not be visible in data. Out of that I compare it to SOPs and strategies that are in place and suggested best practices. I them present all of this to an AI and ask it to combine this and come of with a unified solution that addresses all of these challenges in the best way. This can result in improved SOPs, better human relationships and ultimately in better understanding.
- Finance: I would effectively take the same approach as for the Operations: you have to analyze, think and understand first. If you first go to the assistant, you will get stuck with that answer. You make decisions.
- Strategy: The same as the ones before....
I all cases the protocol must be: "You think. That AI can assist. You make decisions."
In all cases the message to your employee must be "Man, I appointed you to think. Go and do that and come up with the best possible solution and then see how you can accelerate or even improve that using an AI tool."
Anyone is else to discuss of disagree my view.

- Step 1: Feed YOUR context (the missing foundation)
Before ANY training, your AI needs to understand:

→ Your industry
→ Your audience

**Pro tip:** Create a "Context Document"

Step 3: Define personality & boundaries
Answer these for your AI:
→ Tone: Formal or conversational? Data-driven or emotional? Provocative or safe?
→ Structure: Short punchy paragraphs? Long-form deep dives? Bullet lists or storytelling?
**Non-negotiables:**
- Never use corporate jargon
- Always cite sources
- Don't oversell—educate first

- Step 4: Train through feedback loops

→ Give AI a small task: "Write a LinkedIn post about AI productivity"

Stop using hypotheticals. Give your AI the actual work you're doing:
- Draft that client proposal

If no → identify the gap → retrain.
Real work = real learning.

Your AI Twin isn't "set and forget."
**Weekly check-ins:**

- New skills needed?
